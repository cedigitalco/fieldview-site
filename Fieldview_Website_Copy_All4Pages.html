Commitment & Cancellation
The Questions We Get Asked Before Anyone Signs.
Do I have to sign a long-term contract?
No. Month-to-month, cancel anytime. We ask for 30 days notice so we can close out your monitoring cleanly, but there's no lock-in, no cancellation fee, and no annual commitment required. We'd rather keep you because the reports are valuable than because you're contractually stuck.
What if the first report isn't what I expected?
Tell us. We'll revise it. Every client's market is slightly different, and the first report is also a calibration exercise — you'll often have context we don't about which competitors actually matter and which signals to prioritize. That feedback makes every subsequent report sharper.
How customized is the report to my specific market?
Fully. We're not distributing a generic industry report to 50 clients. Every report is built specifically around your named competitors, your geography, your pricing tier, and your stated strategic priorities. If you want us to track a specific competitor or signal, tell us and we'll add it.
Can I share the report with my leadership team?
Yes. The report is yours. Share it internally however you'd like. If you need to share it with your franchisor or a business partner, that's fine too. The only thing we ask is that you don't distribute it publicly or to competitors — the intelligence in it is specific to your market.
How soon will I receive my first report?
Within 30 days of your start date. We typically have it in your hands in 3 weeks. We move quickly on the first report because we know you want to see what you're actually getting before the billing cycle repeats.
What do you do between reports?
We're monitoring continuously. If something significant happens in your competitive landscape between report cycles — a competitor makes a major move, a new player enters your market, a pricing shift we'd flag as urgent — we'll send you a note. You won't hear from us constantly, but you won't be caught off guard either.

What I Believe
The Philosophy Behind the Work.

These aren't values for a pitch deck. They're the things that actually shape how I build every report.

  • Operators deserve the same intelligence that big companies have. A PE-backed competitor has a strategy team, a research budget, and a war room. You have 15 minutes between jobs. That shouldn't determine who has better market information.
  • A report that sits unread is worthless. Every Fieldview report is written to be read by a busy operator in 30 minutes, not a consultant who has all day. If it can't pass that test, I rewrite it.
  • One good recommendation beats ten vague insights. I've read too many market research reports that tell you a lot and recommend nothing. Every Fieldview report ends with one specific, actionable recommendation — with rationale, estimated impact, and a practical path to execute.
  • The relationship compounds in value over time. The first report is a snapshot. By the fourth quarter, you have a year of documented competitive history for your market. That's a different thing entirely — and it's almost impossible to replicate if you let the gap grow.
  • Trust is built by being honest, not by being confident. If something in your market is uncertain, I'll say it's uncertain. If a competitor made a move I can't explain yet, I'll flag it as a signal to watch, not spin a theory. You can only make good decisions from accurate information.
